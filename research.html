Research Interests

A.   Threat Intelligence - Cyberattacks cause loss of critical corporate data, compromise system integrity, and disrupt operational infrastructures. Threat intelligence allows prevention and mitigation of existing or emerging cyberattacks by providing security researchers with data and context about attackers, their motivation and capabilities, and indicators of compromise on the attacked platform. However, raw data and information contained in threat intelligence reports is often mislabeled as intelligence, and the processes and motives for producing threat intelligence are often misconstrued. The aim of this research is to:

  • Assign trust and confidence scores to attack related knowledge at varying levels of granularity based on provenance and other deep probabilistic graphical modeling approaches.
  • Create deep learning models for predicting malware behavior and help investigate past and future attacks, and fill gaps or identify anomalies in collected information through dynamic graph anomaly detection, link prediction and other novel approaches.
Role - Primary Investigator, Funding Agency - IBM, Dollar Amount - $350,000
Investigators: Dr. Mohammad Zaki, Dr. Alex Gittens, Dr. Charu Aggarwal (IBM).
Ph.D. Student - Sharmishtha Dutta.
Undergrads - Destin Lee, Qicheng Ma, Chuqiao Gu (Qiao), Sean Hale, Jared Gridley

B.   Healthcare Analytics (HEALS) - The primary goal of the HEALS (Health Empowerment by Analytics, Learning, and Semantics) project is to apply advanced cognitive computing capabilities to help people understand and improve their own health conditions. In particular, we are exploring areas including personalized and mobile medical care, improved healthcare analytics, and new data-based approaches to driving down the cost of medical care.
